## Essential Resume Sections: What You Need to Know

Creating an effective resume starts with including five must-have sections that recruiters expect to see, along with [five optional sections](https://www.lockedinai.com/blog/additional-resume-sections-to-make-an-impact) to further enhance your application. These sections form the backbone of your resume, presenting a clear, organized overview of your qualifications and experience.

In this guide, we’ll break down each section, explain how to craft them effectively, and provide examples to help you stand out.

## Key Resume Sections

### 1. Contact Information

The header is the first thing recruiters notice. It’s typically highlighted in a larger font or different color to grab attention. Ensure this section includes:

- **Your full name, job title, and location:** This helps recruiters quickly identify you and understand your geographic fit for the role.

- **Professional email address and phone number:** Avoid informal email addresses—use one that’s professional.

- **LinkedIn profile or portfolio link:** These provide recruiters with more insight into your professional history and work samples.

_Pro tip_: Avoid including a photo to maintain focus on your qualifications and prevent bias.

### 2. Resume Summary or Objective

Recruiters spend an average of seven seconds scanning a resume, so it’s critical to grab their attention quickly. A strong **summary** or **objective** can make all the difference.

- **Resume Summary:** A concise 3-4 sentence overview of your professional experience, achievements, and key skills. Ideal for experienced candidates.

_Example:_

**Summary**

Accomplished Biomedical Researcher with over 8 years of experience conducting cutting-edge research in molecular biology and cellular engineering. Skilled in designing and executing experiments to investigate disease mechanisms, with a proven track record of publishing in high-impact journals. Expertise in CRISPR gene editing, flow cytometry, and bioinformatics analysis. Recognized for innovative problem-solving and leading collaborative projects that advance scientific knowledge and contribute to the development of novel therapeutics.

- **Resume Objective:** A brief 1-3 sentence statement highlighting your career goals and how they align with the role. Perfect for entry-level candidates or career changers.

_Example:_

**Objective**

Results-driven Sales Manager seeking to leverage 8+ years of experience in driving revenue growth and building high-performing sales teams at Summit Enterprises. Aiming to apply strong leadership, strategic planning, and relationship-building skills to exceed sales targets, enhance customer satisfaction, and contribute to the company’s overall success.

### 3. Work Experience

This is the most important section of your resume, showcasing your qualifications through real-world examples. It should be **targeted** and **results-driven**:

- **Targeted:** Tailor this section to match the job description, using relevant keywords to align your experience with the employer’s needs.

- **Results-driven:** Highlight measurable achievements and the impact of your work in previous roles. Use action verbs and quantify results wherever possible.

_Example Entry:_

**Digital Marketing Specialist**

Creative Media Solutions | Chicago, IL

_02/2018 – 11/2022_

- Increased website traffic by 30% through SEO optimization and content marketing strategies.

- Managed a $20,000 monthly PPC budget, improving ROI by 35%.

- Led social media campaigns across platforms, boosting engagement by 25%.

This approach makes your resume both ATS-friendly and appealing to hiring managers.

### 4. Skills Section

The skills section provides a quick overview of your core competencies. It’s especially helpful for recruiters who skim resumes for relevant qualifications.

- **Hard skills:** Technical abilities like programming languages, data analysis, or graphic design.

- **Soft skills:** Personal attributes like leadership, communication, or time management.

_Example Skills Section:_

- Proficient in Python and SQL

- Strong problem-solving and critical thinking skills

- Experienced in Agile project management

When listing skills, align them with the job description to maximize relevance.

### 5. Education Section

Your education section highlights your academic background and any formal qualifications. For recent graduates or those changing careers, this section is particularly important.

**What to include:**

- **Degree:** Start with the highest degree earned (e.g., Bachelor’s, Master’s).

- **Institution:** Name of the university or college.

- **Location:** City and state (or country if international).

- **Graduation date:** Include the year, or expected date if still studying.

- **Honors and awards:** List distinctions like Dean’s List or scholarships.

- **Relevant coursework:** Highlight coursework that applies to the job, especially if you’re a recent graduate.

_Example Entry:_

**Bachelor of Science in Computer Science**

University of California, Los Angeles | Los Angeles, CA

Graduated: May 2022

- GPA: 3.8/4.0

- Relevant Coursework: Data Structures, Machine Learning, Cloud Computing

## Final Thoughts

These essential sections form the foundation of a strong resume. Tailor each section to the specific job, keeping content clear, concise, and relevant. With a polished and targeted resume, you’ll increase your chances of making a lasting impression and landing your next opportunity.
